Ludhiana city is situated on the banks of river Satluj, which is one of the five rivers viz. Satluj, Raavi, Chenab, Beas and Jehlum, from which Punjab got its name. Literally the word Punjab means 'Land of five rivers'
This city was founded in the time of Lodhi dynasty which ruled in Delhi from 1451-1526 A.D. It was originally known as Lodhi-ana, which means the town of Lodhi’s. The name later changed to the present name Ludhiana.

    Bhai Randhir Singh Nagar

    This is probably biggest planned colony in entire Punjab. This is further divided into 10 blocks named as A, B, C .... up to J block.

    Aggar Nagar

    This is located on Ludhiana-Ferozepur highway. It is divided into two parts named as Sector A and Sector B and both parts are located across the road.

    Field Ganj

    This is situated in old part of the city near bridge near railway station. This bridge is called Jagraon Bridge. Field Ganj is a major business centre of clothes, cosmetics, readymades etc.

    Industrial Area

    Ludhiana is a major industrial city of Punjab or you can say of North India. A number of industrial clusters are situated here. The industrialisation of the city started with two areas of the city named as Industrial area A and Industrial Area B
